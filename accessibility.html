Web Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: October 28, 2025

♿
Our Commitment: WFY Solutions is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards.

Our Accessibility Standards

We strive to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA standards issued by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities and more usable for everyone.

Conformance Status

WCAG 2.1 Level AA Conformance: We aim to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards. This means:

  • Partially Conformant: Some parts of the content may not fully conform to the accessibility standards
  • Ongoing Effort: We are actively working to achieve full conformance
  • Regular Testing: We conduct regular accessibility audits and improvements

Measures to Support Accessibility

WFY Solutions takes the following measures to ensure accessibility:

Organizational Commitment

  • Include accessibility as part of our design and development process
  • Set internal accessibility standards and guidelines
  • Provide accessibility training for our team
  • Include people with disabilities in our user testing when possible

Technical Implementation

  • Semantic HTML structure for screen reader compatibility
  • Proper heading hierarchy for easy navigation
  • Descriptive link text (avoiding "click here")
  • Alternative text for images
  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios
  • Resizable text without loss of functionality
  • Responsive design for various devices and screen sizes

Accessibility Features

Perceivable

Information and user interface components are presented in ways that users can perceive:

  • Text Alternatives: Alternative text provided for non-text content
  • Adaptable Content: Content can be presented in different ways without losing meaning
  • Distinguishable: High contrast between text and background for easy reading
  • Color: Information is not conveyed by color alone

Operable

User interface components and navigation are operable:

  • Keyboard Accessible: All functionality available via keyboard
  • Enough Time: Users have enough time to read and use content
  • Seizures: Content does not cause seizures or physical reactions
  • Navigable: Clear navigation and page structure
  • Input Modalities: Support for various input methods beyond keyboard

Understandable

Information and operation of user interface are understandable:

  • Readable: Text is readable and understandable
  • Predictable: Web pages appear and operate in predictable ways
  • Input Assistance: Help users avoid and correct mistakes
  • Clear Language: Content written in clear, simple language

Robust

Content is robust enough to be interpreted reliably by a wide variety of user agents, including assistive technologies:

  • Compatible: Compatible with current and future assistive technologies
  • Valid Code: Clean, validated HTML and CSS
  • ARIA Labels: Proper use of ARIA (Accessible Rich Internet Applications) where appropriate

Assistive Technologies Supported

Our website is designed to work with:

  • Screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack)
  • Screen magnification software
  • Speech recognition software
  • Keyboard-only navigation
  • Alternative input devices

Browser and Device Compatibility

Our website is compatible with:

  • Modern web browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
  • Desktop computers, laptops, tablets, and smartphones
  • Various operating systems (Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, Linux)

Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some accessibility limitations may exist:

  • Third-party embedded content may not be fully accessible
  • Legacy content may not meet current standards
  • PDF documents may have limited accessibility (we're working to improve this)
  • Some dynamic content may present challenges for certain assistive technologies

We are actively working to address these limitations and welcome your feedback.

Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is an ongoing effort. We are committed to:

  • Regular accessibility audits and testing
  • Addressing identified issues promptly
  • Implementing user feedback
  • Staying current with accessibility standards and best practices
  • Testing with real users who use assistive technologies
  • Updating content and features with accessibility in mind

Documents and Downloads

We strive to make documents and downloadable content accessible:

  • Documents are provided in accessible formats where possible
  • PDF documents include proper tags and structure
  • Alternative formats available upon request
  • Clear descriptions of document content

Third-Party Content

Our website may include content from third-party services (social media, analytics, etc.). While we cannot fully control the accessibility of third-party content, we:

  • Select vendors with strong accessibility commitments
  • Minimize reliance on inaccessible third-party content
  • Provide alternatives where possible
  • Advocate for improved accessibility with our vendors

Feedback and Contact

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of our website. If you encounter accessibility barriers, please let us know:

To Report Accessibility Issues:

  • LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/wfyoung2ai
  • Include: Description of the issue, page URL, browser/assistive technology used

We aim to respond to accessibility feedback within 5 business days and to propose a solution within 10 business days.

Alternative Access

If you cannot access certain content or features:

  • Contact us for an accessible alternative
  • Request information in an alternative format
  • Ask for assistance with specific tasks

We will work with you to provide the information or functionality you need in an accessible way.

Legal Compliance

We strive to comply with:

  • Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act
  • European Accessibility Act (EAA)
  • Other applicable accessibility laws and regulations

Formal Complaints

If you are not satisfied with our response to your accessibility concern:

  • You may escalate your concern through additional communication
  • You have the right to file a complaint with relevant regulatory bodies
  • We will cooperate fully with any formal accessibility reviews
